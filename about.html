Legend of the Goblins of the Enchanted Cellar

When touring the surroundings of La Bodega Encantada, located on 35 Chacabuco street, you certainly feel a different atmosphere. Former property of the Coll family, this building knew how to see how the delicious wine was prepared that would brighten the singing of local artists and also of anyone who let themselves be carried away by its seductive charm. With the passing of the years, a mantle of oblivion and abandonment covered the cellar and above it, first as whispers and then as an open secret, mysterious stories made their presence in its empty rooms. The residents of Cafayate baptized it with this iconic title, assuring that the place was regularly frequented by children to have adventures with goblins and ghosts. The story grew healthily between the afternoon conversations with the aroma of cooked mate and buns until they ripped the air on the nights of the Serenade to Cafayate that took place in this old property. February and the carnival welcomed them with a new air and the serenading leprechauns began another song. Immortalized in the famous murals of the winery, they are responsible for the mischief, revelry and joy of the party. During these unforgettable evenings you can listen to how singers and ballads invoke them in their lyrics, eager to share with them, or perhaps afraid of not forgetting them, the fervor of carnival.

See the past Serenatas of Cafayate

For three nights, La Bodega Encantada becomes the meeting point for thousands of people, both from Salta and from the entire country. There, you can enjoy a true cultural display where singers and poets loudly celebrate their joy together with the legendary elves of folklore.
